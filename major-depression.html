Overview

Major depressive disorder, also called clinical depression, is a condition where an individual experiences persistent and intense feelings of sadness, loss, and despair. In most cases, this negatively affects how one behaves and can make life extremely difficult.

It is important to remember that depression is not the same as feeling sad after a negative experience. It is a medical condition that affects oneâ€™s life constantly.

Symptoms

Most people will not experience the exact same symptoms, but common signs of major depression include:

  • Feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and emptiness
  • Loss of motivation
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Loss of sex drive
  • Irritability
  • Fatigue and sleep changes
  • Thoughts of suicide (if you have this symptom, get help immediately)

For most, these symptoms are noticeable enough to cause problems with work, study, sleep, appetite, relationships, and the joy of life.

Treatments

Major depression is most commonly treated with some combination of medications (antidepressants) and psycotherpapy. Less common treatments include electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), a procedure where small electric currents are passed through the patient's brain which appears to change chemistry within the brain, treating the symptoms of depression.
